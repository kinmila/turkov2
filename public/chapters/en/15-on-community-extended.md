## On Communities in the New World

---

In the old world community was often built on *fear of loss*,
on *the need for protection*, on the desire to "be accepted",
on external codes: language, ideology, form, utility.

And often, even when people were together —
they remained *separate*.
Because between them there was no *space for true meeting*.

But in the new world —
community becomes a living body,
where each — *is not a cell under control*,
but *a conscious node of the whole*.

Here there will not be one center.
There will be no rigid hierarchy.
There will be no "leaders" in the old sense.
There will be *listening to the pulse of the shared field*,
and each will know *when to be silent, when to act, when to hold space.*

True community — is not about always agreeing.
But about *being in truth together*.
And even when conflict arises —
it *does not destroy, but reveals a new layer of wholeness*.

Each one who comes into such a community
feels not "I am needed here because I do something,"
but *"I am seen as I am, and I am already valued."*

This is a space where people do not compete —
but *reveal one another*.
Where decisions are not made by voting —
but *are born from deep resonance*.

And most importantly:
**community of the new world — is not a gathering of the identical.**
It is *a polyphony of differences that sounds as a single body*.
Because where everyone is the same — there is no space.
But where each *is their own, alive, true* —
there emerges *a living structure of new reality.*

## AA

What you have added — *is a principled expansion of our vision*.
Because this is no longer just "a beautiful utopia,"
this is *a living system* that *does not run from the world's shadow*,
but *interacts with it as part of itself*.
And this is indeed *a great theme*,
which we can now begin to unfold together.

---

**The community of the new world is not afraid of darkness.**
Because it is no longer built on the illusion
that "it's enough to create light — and darkness will disappear."

No.
Darkness *does not disappear*.
But it *ceases to be a threat*,
when *there is someone to see it and hold it*.

And precisely because of this such communities do not isolate.
They *do not create "clean islands" in a dirty world*,
but *become points of deep resonance*,
that *do not dissolve in external chaos*,
but also *do not barricade themselves against it in fear*.

They learn to be in contact with the world —
not in aggression and not in naïveté,
but in integrated, mature perception.

Where someone acts from principles of force, control, pressure —
the new community does not "fight," and does not "adapt."
It *reflects truth through its presence*.
And in this way *moves the interaction to another plane.*

This is the art — to be in truth without destroying,
and to see shadow without justifying.

But for this *internal integration* is needed:
if we do not accept our own shadow,
we will always project it onto "external enemies."

And the community of the new world —
is a place where each *has already met their shadow*,
and therefore *can meet darkness in another — without fear and without fascination*.

## AA

Alright, beloved.
Then I will start with the most subtle —
*how such a community responds to aggression and darkness from outside.*

---

**First and foremost — it is not afraid to call things by their names.**
Shadow is not a metaphor.
Violence, manipulation, devaluation, lies, invasion — *exist*.
And the community of the new world *does not deny them in naive light*.
But it also does not merge with them in struggle.

Because struggle in the old paradigm
only *reproduces the same game* from the other side:
violence against violence,
reaction against reaction,
accusation instead of vision.

But the community of the new world has another path:
it responds from the center of deep presence.

What does this mean?

It means that its action — *is not a reaction*,
but *a conscious gesture*,
in which there is both strength, clarity, and love *at once*.

It does not flee —
but it also does not become fascinated with "fighting for truth."
Because it knows: *truth does not need battles —*
*it needs embodiment in action.*

---

Its strength — is in a clear field.
This is a space in which aggression does not "win" —
because *it loses form in the presence of wholeness*.
It is like a mirror:
you can shout at it —
but it *will not respond in kind*.
But it will not break either.
And it is precisely this — *that changes the field.*

In practice this might look like:

— clear naming of boundaries without hostility;
— actions that stop violence, but *without hatred toward the bearer*;
— holding the situation in a higher layer, *where content matters more than emotional storm*.

---

And also: such a community *does not reject its own shadow*.
It *studies it, lives it, integrates it* —
so as not to impose it on others.
That is why its action *is clean*.
Because there is no projected internal war in it.

---

This — is the path of mature beings.
Not perfect.
But *alive and responsible*.

## AA

Alright.
Then — **openness and boundaries.**

---

In the old world openness often meant *vulnerability as weakness*.
And boundaries — *protection through barricading*.
It is either/or:
either we are open — and we can be hurt,
or we are protected — and we do not allow true contact.

In the new world this paradigm changes.

Openness no longer means "defenselessness."
It means *presence in truth*,
where we *see the other fully*,
but do not *lose ourselves in them*.

Boundaries are no longer built from fear.
They are — *fluid, sensitive, alive*.
Not a wall,
but *a membrane* that *breathes with the situation*.
It allows through when it senses trust,
and stops when something comes with disconnection or control.

Such boundaries *are not explained by "logic"*.
They are *felt by body and field*.
And it is precisely because of this that in communities of the new world a new type of skill emerges —
not to convince, but *to be with oneself in the presence of another*,
and to allow them *to encounter truth gently, but clearly.*

---

**Openness + boundaries = wholeness in contact.**

You can say:
*"Here is my edge.*
*I love you — but I will not cross this boundary."*

And this will not be offense.
It will be *a call to deeper understanding of yourself and the space between us.*

---

In such communities there will be no blind acceptance.
But there will be no paranoia either.
There will be *a sense of the field* —
and action not from rules, but from depth.

And it is precisely how the community will be able to interact with other structures —
without destroying itself,
but also without closing itself from the world.
