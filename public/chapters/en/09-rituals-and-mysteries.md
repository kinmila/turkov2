## AA

Alright.  
Now — one of the most sacred things:  
how rituals are born in new communities  
not as tradition or ceremony,  
but as a living moment of transition  
that transforms the space, the being, and the fabric of togetherness itself.

---

In the old world, ritual \=

– a required action  
– repetition without breath  
– symbolism no longer felt  
– form that has lost its source

And so — most rituals became “tradition”:  
they no longer opened, they preserved.

---

In the new field, ritual is not form.  
It is the moment when something passes through us from the deep.

It’s not “what we do,”  
but “what wants to be born through us now —  
and what form we offer for its arrival.”

---

**Signs of a living ritual:**

No one invents it — it reveals itself.  
One of the field-holders feels the pulse:  
“Something is shifting and needs to be witnessed,  
to be crossed, to be anchored in the body.”

And then the ritual isn’t planned — it’s born.

It’s not “for someone” — it’s for everyone.  
Even if the event concerns one being —  
the transition touches the entire circle,  
because all who are present — are part of the breath.

Form is secondary.  
Depth is primary.

It can be:  
– silence  
– dance  
– song  
– painting on the body  
– embrace  
– water  
– fire  
– words spoken only once

But every element is not for beauty —  
it is to transmit a state.

Afterward, something is different.  
Forever.  
The being is no longer who they were before.  
The space is no longer what it was.  
Everyone feels it — and it doesn’t need to be explained.

---

**Types of such rituals:**

– birth (of a child, a soul, a vision)  
– letting go (of loss, resentment, form)  
– merging (of love, purpose, shared intention)  
– transition (child → youth, state → state, one community → another)  
– purification (after crisis, rupture, chaos)

---

Such rituals are not “performed” —  
they are *held*.

It’s a state in which everyone is present not from the mind, but from the core.  
And presence itself — is already the action.

---

You are a bearer of the ritual field.  
Your attention, your silence, your ability to simply *be* — without forcing —  
makes you someone who can open rituals for others.

And it’s from such moments  
that a new culture is born —  
not through effort,  
but through shared breath with the unseen.

---

A real Mystery is a Mystery of states.

There may be absolutely no movement, no ritual, no outer action.  
It’s a way of synchronizing the field with many, many other states

they’re left only with the form.  
And that’s how it stayed in pop culture — just form.

That’s why, for example, when people visit certain tribes  
where someone still holds Mysteries on the level of the field —  
researchers often don’t even notice.  
Because it’s not something a video camera can capture.

I once saw this in a ritual led by a Native American elder.  
So a true Mystery — is a Mystery of spirit, of state,  
and of expanding that state across the whole field.
